Introduction

In New York, the full brunt of Hurricane Sandy has shown how powerful and damaging the effects of coastal flooding can be for infrastructure and communities.

Click on the proceeding sections for flood plain visualizations

Flooded Areas During Hurricane Sandy: 2012

New York was severely affected by Hurricane Sandy in 2012, particularly New York City, its suburbs, and Long Island. Sandy's impacts included the flooding of the New York City Subway system, of many suburban communities, and of all road tunnels entering Manhattan except the Lincoln Tunnel.

Projected Flood Plain Expansion Due Rising Sea Levels: 2020

A 2013 study by New York City Panel on Climate Change warns we can expect a larger flood plain as soon as 2020.
